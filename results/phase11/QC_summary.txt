Phase 11: Metabolite Data Quality Control Summary

The metabolomics dataset initially contained 156 samples and 136 metabolites. Metabolite-level missingness was assessed, and metabolites with more than 20% missing values were designated for removal. No metabolites exceeded this threshold.

Sample-level missingness was subsequently calculated across the remaining metabolites. Samples with more than 20% missing metabolite values were designated for exclusion. No samples exceeded the sample missingness threshold.

Metabolites with zero or near-zero variance (variance ≤ 1 × 10⁻⁸) were assessed and excluded as uninformative features. No metabolites met this exclusion criterion.

The QC-passed metabolite values were standardized using z-score scaling to produce features with a mean of approximately zero and a standard deviation of one. This ensures comparability between metabolites measured on different scales.

After quality control, the final dataset retained all 156 samples and 136 metabolites.

QC thresholds:

* Metabolite missingness: >20%
* Sample missingness: >20%
* Near-zero variance: ≤1 × 10⁻⁸
* Normalization: z-score standardization

Final QC dataset:

* Samples before QC: 156
* Samples after QC: 156
* Metabolites before QC: 136
* Metabolites after QC: 136
