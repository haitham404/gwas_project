## Phase 13 Results

The performance of Logistic Regression and Random Forest was compared using Accuracy, Sensitivity, Specificity, Balanced Accuracy, and AUC.

| Model               | Accuracy | Sensitivity | Specificity | Balanced Accuracy |   AUC |
| ------------------- | -------: | ----------: | ----------: | ----------------: | ----: |
| Logistic Regression |    0.531 |       0.550 |       0.500 |             0.525 | 0.525 |
| Random Forest       |    0.969 |       1.000 |       0.917 |             0.958 | 1.000 |

Random Forest substantially outperformed Logistic Regression across all evaluation metrics. Logistic Regression showed weak classification performance and produced convergence and rank-deficiency warnings, indicating model instability due to the large number of predictors relative to the available sample size.

In contrast, Random Forest correctly identified all diabetic individuals and misclassified only one non-diabetic individual. The model achieved an accuracy of 96.9%, a balanced accuracy of 95.8%, and an AUC of 1.000.

Feature importance analysis identified 1,5-anhydroglucitol and mannose as the strongest predictors. Citrulline, enyl-palmitoyl-GPC, palmitoylcholine, gluconate, and glucose were also among the most important features.

Overall, Random Forest was the best-performing classification model. However, the near-perfect performance should be interpreted cautiously and validated using cross-validation or an independent dataset to assess model generalizability.
